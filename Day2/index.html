Phishing Email Analysis Report

Objective: Identify and analyze phishing characteristics in a suspicious email sample.

Simulated Phishing Email Sample

From: <security.alerts@wellsfargo-update.net>
To: John Doe <johndoe@example.com>
Date: Mon, 23 Sep 2024 10:30:00 -0400
Subject: Urgent Security Alert: Your Account is Suspended
Dear Valued Wells Fargo Customer, We have detected suspicious activity on your account and have temporarily suspended it for your security. To restore full access to your account and avoid permanent suspension, please click the secure link below and follow the on-screen instructions to verify your identity. Login and Restore Your Account Failure to act within 24 hours will result in the permanent closure of your account. We apologize for any inconvenience this may have cause. Sincerely, Wells Fargo Security Team

Phishing Indicators Found

A detailed analysis reveals several key indicators of a phishing attempt, as outlined in the task hints.

  • Sender's Email Address for Spoofing

    The email is sent from security.alerts@wellsfargo-update.net. This is a clear case of email spoofing and domain impersonation. While it includes the company name "wellsfargo," the domain is wellsfargo-update.net, not the legitimate wellsfargo.com. This is designed to deceive the recipient into believing the email is from a trusted source.

  • Suspicious Links

    The link provided, http://wellsfargo.com.security-verify.pw/login.php, is suspicious. Although the text says "wellsfargo.com," the actual top-level domain is .pw, and the hostname is security-verify.pw. The use of "wellsfargo.com" as a subdomain is a common tactic to trick users. Hovering over the link reveals this discrepancy, which is a major red flag.

  • Urgent or Threatening Language

    The email uses strong, urgent language to pressure the recipient into acting without thinking. Phrases like "Urgent Security Alert," "Your Account is Suspended," and "Failure to act within 24 hours will result in the permanent closure of your account" are examples of social engineering. This tactic aims to create fear and bypass the recipient's critical judgment.

  • Spelling and Grammar Errors

    The email contains a grammatical error: "We apologize for any inconvenience this may have cause." The correct word should be "caused." The generic salutation "Dear Valued Wells Fargo Customer" instead of a personalized name is also a common indicator of a phishing attempt, as legitimate companies often use the user's name.

Conclusion and Recommended Actions

This email is a clear phishing attempt designed to steal credentials. The indicators found include a spoofed sender address, deceptive links, a sense of urgency, and grammatical errors. The user should not click the link or reply to the email. The appropriate action would be to delete the email or report it to the IT or security department. This awareness of phishing tactics is crucial for effective threat analysis.
